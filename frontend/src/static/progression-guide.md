# Double progression (with RIR)

**Double progression** (from `plans/wip.md`): add reps until the top of the prescribed rep range, then add load and reset to the bottom. **RIR** is separate: it describes how hard each set is (~RIR 2 on compounds, ~RIR 1 on isolations in this program), not when to change weight.

---

## Rule in one line

Hold weight steady and build reps to the **top** of the bracket; then increase load and drop reps back toward the **bottom**. Repeat.

---

## Concrete example: Incline DB press

From the program: **3 sets · 8–12 reps · ~RIR 2** (Upper A / Upper B).

- **Bottom of range:** 8 reps  
- **Top of range:** 12 reps  
- **Progression trigger:** hit **12, 12, 12** on all working sets at the current dumbbells, then go up (e.g. +5 lb per hand if that’s the smallest jump).

### Phase 1 — Same weight, reps climb

Open with a weight where **8 reps** leaves about **2 reps in reserve** (RIR 2). Example: **50 lb** dumbbells.

| Session | DBs | Set 1 | Set 2 | Set 3 | Notes |
|--------|-----|-------|-------|-------|--------|
| 1 | 50 | 8 | 8 | 8 | At bottom of 8–12; keep weight. |
| 2 | 50 | 9 | 8 | 8 | Add reps where you can. |
| 3 | 50 | 9 | 9 | 8 | |
| 4 | 50 | 9 | 9 | 9 | |
| 5 | 50 | 10 | 9 | 9 | |
| 6 | 50 | 10 | 10 | 9 | |
| 7 | 50 | 10 | 10 | 10 | |
| 8 | 50 | 11 | 10 | 10 | |
| 9 | 50 | 11 | 11 | 10 | |
| 10 | 50 | 11 | 11 | 11 | |
| 11 | 50 | 12 | 11 | 11 | First set at 12 — not done yet. |
| 12 | 50 | 12 | 12 | 11 | |
| 13 | 50 | **12** | **12** | **12** | **All sets at top** → add load. |

Sessions won’t always move one rep forward; repeating a week is fine. The idea is **fixed DBs until 12, 12, 12**.

### Phase 2 — Add load, reps reset

| Session | DBs | Set 1 | Set 2 | Set 3 |
|--------|-----|-------|-------|-------|
| 14 | **55** | **8** | **8** | **8** (or 8, 8, 7) |

Heavier weight → back toward the **bottom** of **8–12**. Then climb **8 → … → 12** again on **55s**.

---

## RIR sanity check

Double progression says **when** to bump weight (all sets at the top of the range). RIR says **whether** the weight is appropriate: if the next step up (e.g. **55s** for 8s) is **RIR 0**, the jump is too big — use a smaller increment if available, or stay at the old weight until **12, 12, 12** feels less like max effort before trying the jump again.

---

## Why “double”

Two staged progressions: **reps up** at the same load, then **load up** with reps reset. Not “twice as fast.”
